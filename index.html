Nike Timeline

Before it was Nike, it was Blue Ribbon Sports (BRS). Founded by University of Oregon track athlete Phil Knight and his coach Bill Bowerman, the company started as a distributor for Japanese shoemaker Onitsuka Tiger (now ASICS). They began with just $1,200 in the bank and sold shoes out of the trunk of Knight’s car.

As the relationship with their Japanese supplier soured, the duo decided to launch their own brand. They settled on the name Nike (the Greek goddess of victory) and debuted the "Swoosh" logo.

Bill Bowerman was obsessed with making a lighter, faster running shoe. Legend has it he poured liquid rubber into his wife’s waffle iron, creating a unique nubbed sole. This "Waffle Trainer" became Nike’s first major breakthrough, proving they weren't just sellers, but inventors.

Nike introduced the Tailwind, the first shoe to feature "Air" technology—pressurized gas inside a flexible urethane bag. This tech, developed by former NASA engineer Frank Rudy, would eventually lead to the visible air bubbles that define the brand’s aesthetic today.

Nike launched the "Just Do It" campaign with a TV ad featuring 80-year-old running icon Walt Stack. The slogan, inspired by the last words of a death row inmate ("Let's do it"), turned Nike into a brand for everyone—not just elite athletes.

Nike went public on December 2, 1980, at 18 cents per share (adjusted for splits). This infusion of capital allowed the brand to expand globally and take on its biggest rival at the time, Adidas.
